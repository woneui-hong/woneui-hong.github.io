## 0. Overview: Why Does the Market Leave AMR 'Cheap'?

Alpha Metallurgical Resources (AMR)'s low multiple (P/FCF 6.2x) is not simply undervalued but reflects the structural fear held by the market. This report lists three core risks that investors are concerned about and analyzes how management is defending against them with specific numbers and strategies.

All figures and citations are based on [AMR 2025 Q3 10-Q disclosure materials](https://www.sec.gov/ix?doc=/Archives/edgar/data/0001704715/000170471525000042/amr-20250930.htm).

---

## 1. Risk 1: Economic Slowdown in Major Export Countries (India, etc.) and Decline in Steel Demand

### [Concern]

Over 70% of AMR's revenue comes from exports, and the company is extremely sensitive to steel demand in Asian markets, including India. If global economic downturn reduces steel production, metallurgical coal prices will inevitably plummet, which directly leads to deteriorating performance.

### [Management's Defense Logic: Flexible Shipments and Cost Control]

Management responds to these concerns with specific operational metrics. Looking at 2025 Q3 results, despite realized prices (Realizations) declining 2.5% year-over-year, the company secured margins by reducing cost per ton (CCS) by 4% ($111.41 → $107.01).

### [Table 1] Operational Metrics Comparison (2024 Q3 vs 2025 Q3)

| Item (Unit: $/Ton) | 2024 Q3 | 2025 Q3 | Change |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Realizations (Realized Price)** | $144.37 | $140.75 | -2.5% |
| **Cost of Coal Sold (Cost)** | $111.41 | $107.01 | **-4.0%** |
| **Gross Margin** | $32.96 | $33.74 | **+2.4%** |

> "Our metallurgical segment's cost of coal sold per ton for the third quarter was $107.01, compared to $111.41 in the prior year period. This improvement reflects our continued focus on operational efficiency and cost management."  
> — *AMR 2025 Q3 Earnings Release*

**Strategic Response:** Management maintains a network of steel customers across more than 25 countries worldwide without depending on specific countries, flexibly allocating shipment volumes. Rather than forcing volumes when demand slows, the company optimizes production focusing on high-margin mines to maintain 'cash flow quality'.

> "We have a diversified customer base across more than 25 countries, which provides us with flexibility in managing our sales mix and mitigating risks associated with regional economic downturns."  
> — *AMR 2025 Q3 Management's Discussion and Analysis*

## 2. Risk 2: ESG and Permanent Exit Fear of the Coal Industry

### [Concern]

Due to carbon-neutral trends, there is fear that the coal industry will become 'Stranded Assets'. The view is that institutional investor capital inflows are restricted, making multiple expansion impossible.

### [Management's Defense Logic: Irreplaceability and Capital Compression]

Management responds to these concerns with two core arguments.

**First, the irreplaceability of metallurgical coal:** Management clearly states that metallurgical coal remains essential for steel production and that there are currently no commercially viable substitutes.

> "Metallurgical coal remains a critical input for steel production, and there are currently no commercially viable substitutes for high-quality met coal in the blast furnace process."  
> — *AMR 2025 Q3 Management's Discussion and Analysis*

**Second, maximizing shareholder value through capital compression:** Rather than being hindered by the situation where external investment is cut off, the company leverages it by pouring all profits into share repurchases, reducing shares outstanding to 12.9 million (approximately 42% of issued shares have already been repurchased). This builds a 'closed-loop' capital structure that amplifies value on its own without external capital infusion.

### [Table 2] Share Repurchase Program Impact

| Item | Value | Notes |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Shares Outstanding (2025-09-30)** | 12,919,308 shares | Based on market cap |
| **Cumulative Treasury Stock Repurchase Cost** | Approximately $2.23 B | Based on Treasury Stock |
| **Repurchase Ratio** | Approximately 42% | Relative to issued shares |
| **3Q 2025 Share Repurchase** | $25.2 M | Quarterly execution |

> "We remain committed to returning capital to shareholders through our share repurchase program, which we believe represents the most attractive investment opportunity available to us."  
> — *AMR 2025 Q3 Earnings Call*

**Environmental Remediation Obligation (ARO) Management:** AMR manages $133.4M in environmental remediation obligations (ARO) as liabilities as of the end of Q3 2025, but this is a level that can be fully paid several times over with cash holdings ($408.5M).

## 3. Risk 3: Volatility of Free Cash Flow (FCF)

### [Concern]

The fact that cumulative FCF for the nine months ended 2025 decreased to $27.8M year-over-year can be interpreted as a slowdown in cash generation. There is concern that dividends or share repurchases may be suspended if coal prices fall when large capital expenditures (CapEx) are needed.

### [Management's Defense Logic: Overwhelming Net Cash Position]

Management responds to these concerns with numerical superiority in financial health.

### [Table 3] Cash Position & Financial Health (as of 2025-09-30)

| Metric | Value | Ratio and Details |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Net Cash Holdings** | **$453.0 M** | Cash($408.5M) + Short-term investments($49.4M) - Total debt($5M) |
| **Free Cash Flow (FCF, 9mo)** | $27.8 M | Operating cash($126.0M) - Capital expenditures($98.2M) |
| **Total Debt** | **$5.0 M** | Principal in current liabilities($2.1M) + Long-term debt($2.9M) |
| **Net Cash / Debt Ratio** | **90.6x** | Overwhelming cash advantage relative to debt |

**Strategic Response:** The current net cash position is a strong buffer that can maintain the current operating scale and minimum share repurchases for years even if FCF temporarily turns negative. Management executed $25.2M in share repurchases during Q3, demonstrating shareholder return commitment with numbers regardless of cash flow volatility.

> "Our strong balance sheet and cash generation capabilities position us well to navigate market volatility while continuing to create value for shareholders."  
> — *AMR 2025 Q3 Earnings Call*

## 4. Synthesis: Reasons Not to Buy vs Reasons to Buy

### [Table 4] Bear Case vs Bull Case Comparison

| Reasons Not to Buy AMR (Bear Case) | Management's Defense Mechanism (Bull Case) |
| :--- | :--- |
| Risk of sharp performance decline during global economic downturn | Strong margin defense capability with cost per ton in the low $100s |
| Blocking of institutional asset inflows due to ESG regulations | Voluntary per-share value increase through share repurchases (EPS leverage) |
| Unstable cash flow due to coal price volatility | Overwhelming net cash holdings of 90x relative to debt |

**Final Judgment:** The market views AMR as a 'coal company that will eventually disappear' and prices it low, but management operates it as a 'capital efficiency machine with inexhaustible cash'. Every time declining demand in export markets depresses the stock price, management takes a paradoxical strategy of repurchasing more shares at cheaper prices, increasing future potential returns.
