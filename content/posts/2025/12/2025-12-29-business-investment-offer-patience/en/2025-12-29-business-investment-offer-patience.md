Through practicing **'meta-routines that improve routines'** in investment study and exercise, I've come to realize something: Why do people with perfect routines hesitate when facing 'business'?

The answer is simple: business and investment operate in **opposite directions of energy**.

## 1. Defining Business and Investment: Offense and Defense

In the game of capitalism, business and investment serve as 'offense' and 'defense' respectively.

| Aspect | Nature | Core Competency | Point of Success |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Business** | **Offense** | **Offer** | When you throw an irresistible proposal |
| **Investment** | **Defense** | **Patience** | When you wait until you cannot help but buy |

## 2. Why Solo Entrepreneurs Must Sell 'Data', Not 'Function'

The most common mistake when designing business routines is obsessing over 'function'. However, the smaller the business, the more impossible it becomes to gain functional advantage.

> **Function is a recipe (Logic); data is aging (Time).**

### The Limits of Function

1. **Function is replicable:** No matter how excellent your web service or app feature, teams with capital and manpower will catch up quickly. (Diminishing marginal utility)
2. **Function loses value over time:** Yesterday's innovation becomes today's standard as technology advances.

### The Exclusivity of Data

1. **Data is exclusive:** If you've systematically collected data in a specific domain (e.g., global corn prices, urgent property listings in a specific region) for a year, no one can recreate 'yesterday's data' today.
2. **Rights create value:** Real estate agents are expensive not because they're friendly (function), but because they have exclusive property information (data) and the right to broker deals.
3. **Time amplifies data's value:** Data becomes more valuable as it accumulates and ages. Historical data becomes irreplicable historical records.

## 3. Business Routines for Value Investors

Just as value investors read endless reports before pressing the buy button, aspiring entrepreneurs endlessly prepare (building websites, designing logos) before throwing offers. But **business revenue only occurs when offers meet the market.**

### ðŸš€ A New 'Meta-Routine' for Entrepreneurs

1. **Internal Routine (Function):** Automate data collection and processing as your private internal system. (Use only as an efficiency tool, not for sale)
2. **External Routine (Offer):** Based on collected data, throw 'irresistible proposals' to the market every week.
3. **Improvement Routine (Meta):** Collect market rejection data to sharpen your offers.

These three routines create a virtuous cycle that reinforces each other. Data accumulated by the internal routine makes the external routine's offers powerful, and feedback from the external routine refines the internal routine through the improvement routine.
